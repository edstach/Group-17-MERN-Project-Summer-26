The flow

Three steps between the check arriving and everyone knowing what they owe.

01

Upload it

Upload a photo of the receipt from your phone or your computer. The AI reads out every line item, the price, and the tax. If the image is not a receipt, it tells you right away instead of guessing.

02

Check it and split it

Scan the lines and correct anything the AI misread. Then choose evenly or by item, putting one person or several on each line depending on who actually shared it.

03

Send it

Everyone gets one clear number. Send the itemized bill out to the table by email or text, and it stays in your history either way.

Features

Built for the awkward part of dinner.

Two ways to split

Evenly when the meal was simple, by item when it was not. Switch between them and the numbers update instantly.

Fix what the AI gets wrong

Thermal paper smudges and fades, and no reader is perfect. Correct any price or name right on the receipt and every total recalculates as you type.

Shared plates, handled

The appetizer everyone picked at goes on everyone who picked at it. Put as many people on a line as you need and its cost divides between them.

Both versions kept

OozeDues stores what the AI first read and the version you corrected. Your history shows what changed, so nothing gets quietly rewritten after the fact.

Tax split in proportion

Order $10 of a $50 bill and you cover a fifth of the tax. On $8 of tax that comes to $1.60, no matter how many people are at the table.

Not a receipt? It says so.

Upload something that is not a receipt and OozeDues returns a clear error rather than inventing numbers that were never there.

Send it to the table

Push the itemized split out to everyone by email or text, so nobody has to screenshot the screen and squint at it.

Your receipts, saved

Open any recent meal and see exactly how it was split and who was on it. At least your last ten stay on file.

Down to the last cent

Three people and a $72.74 check will not divide evenly, and neither will a plate shared three ways. OozeDues hands each leftover cent to one person and tells you who got it, so the parts always add back up to the total exactly.
